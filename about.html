About

A development career built on one conviction: those closest to a problem understand it best.

I'm a development practitioner based in Kano, Nigeria, with over seven years of experience designing, funding and scaling programmes that expand economic, educational and leadership opportunities for young people and women in fragile and underserved communities.

My path into this work started early. My family experienced displacement during a period of communal unrest in my hometown of Okene when I was young, and watching my mother rebuild a livelihood from very little left a lasting impression on how I think about vulnerability and recovery. It's part of why I believe programmes work best when they're shaped with the people they're meant to serve, not just for them — and it grounds the way I approach every project I lead today.

That conviction has carried me from early hands-on programme delivery into senior leadership. I currently serve as Director of Programmes at Bridge Connect Africa Initiative (BCAI), where I oversee a multi-sector portfolio spanning education, livelihoods, social protection, gender and youth development — reaching more than 40,000 people across eleven projects and over $500,000 in mobilised funding. In 2025, I founded YouthXcel Africa, a youth-centred organisation building an inclusive empowerment ecosystem that has already connected over 1,500 young people to learning, income and growth opportunities.

Alongside programme leadership, I sit on global advisory panels — including as a Senior Youth Panelist for the EU Youth Empowerment Fund, where I've contributed to reviewing thousands of youth-led proposals, and as a ONE Africa Champion advocating for stronger development financing across the continent. I studied Mass Communication at Bayero University Kano, graduating in the top 3% of my class, and I'm currently pursuing an MSc in International Development at Ahmadu Bello University.

I'm particularly interested in how organisations grow — how programmes are structured, how funding aligns with strategy, and how the decisions made today shape sustainability tomorrow. I'm open to conversations on programme strategy, resource mobilisation and advisory work across the development sector.
